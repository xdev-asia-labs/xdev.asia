A BA who is technically brilliant but can't persuade stakeholders = project failure. A BA who is great at everything but can't interview = no opportunity to prove it. AI simulation is how you practice with feedback, at no cost, with no limit on repetitions.

---

## 1. Stakeholder Simulation with AI

### 1.1 Why Simulation Works

Difficult stakeholders have recurring patterns:
- **The Skeptic:** "Do we really need AI? Manual works just fine."
- **The Over-requester:** "Just add this feature too — it won't take long, right?"
- **The Risk-avoider:** "What if AI gets it wrong? I'm not signing off on this."
- **The Budget Holder:** "Is this ROI guaranteed? How was it calculated?"
- **The Technical Champion:** Wants AI to do everything; doesn't bother listening to user needs

Practicing with AI simulation → recognize patterns → handle them better in real situations.

### 1.2 Prompt Template for Stakeholder Simulation

**Setup prompt:**
```
You are [stakeholder name], [job title] at [company in industry X].
Your characteristics:
- [Characteristic 1: e.g., "Always asks about ROI and numbers before committing"]
- [Characteristic 2: e.g., "Skeptical about AI because a previous AI project failed"]  
- [Characteristic 3: e.g., "Has budget authority but is not tech-savvy"]

I (the BA) will present an AI feature proposal to you.
Please react in character, ask hard questions, and push back when needed.
Start with: "OK, I have 20 minutes. Tell me what this feature is."
```

**Scenario example:**
```
Scenario: I need to convince the CFO to approve a $50K budget for an AI chatbot.
The CFO has rejected 2 previous AI proposals for "not showing clear ROI."
I have 15 minutes and 5 slides.
```

### 1.3 Types of Simulation Sessions

**Session Type 1: Discovery Meeting**
```
Scenario: New stakeholder, first meeting to elicit requirements.
Goal: Learn how to open the conversation, ask open-ended questions, 
       listen actively, and avoid leading questions.
```

**Session Type 2: Pushback Meeting**
```
Scenario: Stakeholder disagrees with your requirement prioritization.
Goal: Learn how to handle objections, stay calm, and negotiate without losing ground.
```

**Session Type 3: Executive Presentation**
```
Scenario: 10-minute slot with a Director to get sign-off for go-live.
Goal: Clarity, confidence, and handling tough Q&A.
```

## 2. Mock Interview with AI

### 2.1 BA Interview Types and Simulation

**Type 1: Behavioral Interview (STAR Format)**

```
Prompt for AI:
"Please interview me for a Senior Business Analyst role specializing in AI. 
Ask 5 behavioral questions and for each:
1. Ask the question
2. After I answer, give specific feedback:
   - Strengths in my answer
   - Weaknesses or gaps
   - Suggestions for improvement using STAR format"
```

**Type 2: Case Study Interview**

```
Prompt for AI:
"Give me a BA case study: 
 A fintech company wants to automate credit approval with AI.
 Ask me questions like a tough interviewer, 
 evaluating how I structure the problem, identify requirements, 
 and handle ambiguity."
```

**Type 3: Technical Depth Check**

```
Prompt for AI:
"Test my BA knowledge of AI. Ask me sequentially about:
1. The difference between precision and recall — when do you use each?
2. How does a BA design a test set for an AI model?
3. Why is human-in-the-loop necessary?
4. How does GDPR Article 22 affect AI feature requirements?
After each answer, give me a score of 1–5 and explain why."
```

## 3. The Feedback Loop: Making Simulation Genuinely Valuable

### 3.1 After Each Simulation Session

```
Prompt to request feedback:
"Now step out of character.
Evaluate my performance in the simulation we just did:
1. Which response or argument was strongest? Why?
2. Where was I weak or missing something?
3. What questions might the stakeholder have asked that I wasn't ready for?
4. 3 things I should improve for next time?"
```

### 3.2 Progress Tracking Template


## 4. Advanced: Role-play with AI to Increase Difficulty

### Increase Difficulty Gradually

**Level 1:** AI plays a cooperative, supportive stakeholder  
**Level 2:** AI plays a skeptical but reasonable stakeholder  
**Level 3:** AI plays a hostile stakeholder with their own agenda  
**Level 4:** AI plays multiple stakeholders simultaneously (conflicting interests)

```
Level 4 Prompt:
"Play TWO stakeholders in the same meeting:
- A: CTO, wants the AI feature ASAP, doesn't care about the business case
- B: CFO, wants clear ROI, skeptical about AI

The two have dynamic tension. I need to convince both of them.
Start the meeting."
```

## Conclusion

AI simulation doesn't replace real experience — but it **multiplies** preparation speed. 10 simulation sessions with AI + 1 real stakeholder meeting = far better results than just 1 real meeting alone.

Practice until you are **bored** with those hard questions — that's when you're ready for the real thing.
